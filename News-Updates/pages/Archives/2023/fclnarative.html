Kindergarten Supplies for Bright Beginnings: Empowering Our Young Learners

A collaborative initiative unfolded as the College of Engineering and the College of Business and Accountancy united in a mission to make a lasting impact on the educational journey of students. Our outreach team ventured to Dr. Jose Tamayo Memorial Elementary School, not only with the purpose of providing essential school supplies but also with the backing of students from the aforementioned colleges who served as the givers in this meaningful endeavor.

The mission was clear - to equip young minds at the elementary school with the necessary tools for learning, fostering an environment conducive to growth. In recognizing the importance of community support and educational upliftment, the collaboration extended beyond elementary education, reaching into higher education with the active participation of the College of Engineering and the College of Business and Accountancy students as the generous contributors to the cause.

MESSAGE OF THE LEADER

A collaborative initiative unfolded as the College of Engineering and the College of Business and Accountancy united in a mission to make a lasting impact on the educational journey of students. The mission was clear - to equip young minds at the elementary school with the necessary tools for learning, fostering an environment conducive to growth. In recognizing the importance of community support and educational upliftment, the collaboration extended beyond elementary education, reaching into higher education with the active participation of the College of Engineering and the College of Business and Accountancy students as the generous contributors to the cause.

PRAYER

Following Ms. Danna's inspirational address, the atmosphere was further enriched by the Opening Prayer led by Ms. Bianca for the 1st batch and Mr. Carlo Joshua for the 2nd batch of students. Their heartfelt invocation set a spiritual foundation for the day, seeking guidance and blessings for the collective endeavor ahead. The prayer resonated with a sense of unity, acknowledging the diverse talents and backgrounds contributing to the success of the "Empowering Futures" Outreach Program.

As the words of the prayer echoed through the venue, it reinforced the shared commitment and purpose that enveloped the participants. The combination of Danna's empowering message and the Opening Prayer by Ms. Bianca and Mr. Carlo Joshua not only marked the beginning of the program but also ignited a collective spirit that would fuel the day's activities with a sense of purpose, community, and shared responsibility.

Short Story

After the Opening Prayer, the program seamlessly transitioned into a captivating Storytelling session, with Ms. Bianca leading a visual exploration of the Filipino tale of Langgam and Tipaklong. Instead of merely narrating, Ms. Bianca treated the audience to a short film, artfully conveying the timeless lesson of perseverance and collaboration through this age-old fable.

In the heartwarming story, Langgam (ant) and Tipaklong (grasshopper) faced adversity, each leveraging their unique strengths. By presenting the tale as a short film, Ms. Bianca seamlessly connected it to real-life situations, drawing parallels to the challenges students might encounter in their educational journey. The visual storytelling served as a powerful metaphor, highlighting the significance of collaboration, determination, and mutual support—values that align with the core principles of education and community engagement.


Ms. Bianca's decision to present the story through a short film not only added a delightful and entertaining dimension to the event but also instilled a deep sense of commitment among the audience. It made everyone more dedicated to the cause of empowering the students at Dr. Jose Tamayo Memorial Elementary School, fostering a collective spirit of togetherness and understanding. This innovative approach set a poignant stage for the day, paving the way for activities centered around empowerment, encouragement, and meaningful community engagement.

Moral of the Story

After Ms. Bianca shared the interesting tale of Langgam and Tipaklong, Ms. Keith for the 1st batch and Mr. Carlo Joshua for the 2nd batch stepped forward to explain the important lesson from the story. The moral of Langgam and Tipaklong is like a timeless guide that says, "Save your money and plan for the future." It's all about being smart with what you have. The story teaches us to work hard and save, just like the ant did, especially when things are going well, so that when tough times come, we have something to rely on.

Ms. Keith also pointed out the importance of using our time wisely and doing things that matter early on. She stressed how these simple habits help build a strong foundation for the future. This lesson fits perfectly with our goal today–to support, encourage, and make a positive impact in our community. It's about being smart with our resources and time so that we can help ourselves and others in the long run

PHOTO OPPORTUNITY

Amidst the distribution, the opportunity for a collective photo op emerged. Students and contributors came together, capturing the heartwarming moments of generosity and community spirit. The photographs served as tangible memories, immortalizing the commitment to education and the collaborative effort that defined the "Empowering Futures" Outreach Program.As the school supplies found their way into the hands of the students, and the cameras clicked to freeze the smiles and camaraderie, it marked not just the end of an eventful day but the beginning of a lasting impact on the educational journey of the students at Dr. Jose Tamayo Memorial Elementary School.
